# Slide 02 — Current Workflow (Discovery)

## Slide Title
How WhatsApp actually works in your business today

## Objective
Understand their real operational reality before you prescribe anything. This slide is mostly for *asking*, not telling. Do not skip or rush it — everything downstream depends on the answers.

## Key Points (on slide)
- Who monitors the WhatsApp number(s), on what device(s), during what hours?
- How many messages come in per day? Peak days? Off hours?
- How are jobs assigned to executives today — by area, by service, by whoever picks up first?
- How are reschedules, delays and complaints handled today?
- Where do leads go if the owner is unavailable?

## Visual
Blank mini-flowchart with 4 unfilled boxes and dotted arrows, titled **"Your current message flow (to fill in together)"**. Draw it live with the client if possible.

## Speaker Notes
"Before I tell you what I think you need, I want to understand what you actually do today. I'll ask a few boring questions — please answer plainly, even if something sounds basic. There are no wrong answers, only incorrect assumptions on my side. Once we have this picture, the rest of the deck either becomes obvious or becomes irrelevant. Let's draw your current flow together."

## Questions to Ask
1. Is there one WhatsApp number, or multiple? Whose phone(s) are they on?
2. Who replies to customers — only you, or do staff have access?
3. On a typical weekday vs a weekend, roughly how many messages come in?
4. When a new booking request comes in, what happens — literally step by step?
5. When a customer complains, what happens — literally step by step?
6. When a customer wants to reschedule, who decides and how do they inform the exec?
7. If you are in a meeting for 2 hours, what happens to the inbound messages?
8. Have you lost a customer in the last month because of a response delay? What happened?

## What to Capture
- Number(s) of WhatsApp accounts in use, and device count.
- Names/roles of anyone else who handles messages (even informally via forwarding).
- Message volume bands: low / medium / high days.
- The 2–3 workflows that break most often.
- One real story of a missed lead or complaint — keep this as ammunition for the ROI argument later.
