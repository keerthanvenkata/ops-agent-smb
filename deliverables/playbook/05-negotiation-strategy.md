# Negotiation Strategy — Hyderabad SMB

Hyderabad SMB owners (especially Telugu-speaking family-run businesses) negotiate. Expect it. Plan for it. Do not be defensive about it — it's the culture, and respectful pushback is expected.

## Before they negotiate — anchor hard

- **Quote ₹45k first.** Walk through Option B in detail. Only then reveal Option A (₹30k) as the "lean" alternative.
- **Show running costs separately and transparently.** This builds the "I'm not ripping you off" frame that Hyderabad SMB owners deeply value.
- **Mention your opportunity cost in passing.** Not as a threat — but a line like "this month I've had to say no to two other projects because I'm committed to taking this one on properly" communicates value without arrogance.
- **Reference peer businesses without naming them.** "I've seen a similar-size outfit in Kondapur do this" — even if loose — makes you feel like a known operator.

## When they push — the moves

### Move 1: "Can you do it for ₹20,000?"

**Don't say:** "Okay, ₹25k final price."
**Do say:** "At ₹20k, I'd have to drop [specific thing] and [specific thing]. Let me show you what that would look like." — and actually describe a legitimately smaller scope (e.g., no status tracking, no escalation logic, owner does routing manually in the group).
**Why:** Anchors the conversation on *scope* rather than *price*. If they accept the smaller scope, great — you're still paid fairly for the work. If they don't, they now understand ₹30k was reasonable.

### Move 2: "Other people are quoting ₹10–15k / my nephew can do it free"

**Don't say:** "They're not as good" — sounds defensive.
**Do say:** "Absolutely, and if they understand WhatsApp Cloud API, AI integration, routing and status — they'll do a good job. What would worry me is if they're planning to scrape the WhatsApp Business app, because that gets the number banned. If the cheaper route uses the official API and they can stand behind 45 days of support, I'd honestly go with them."
**Why:** You reframe as a technical-risk conversation, not a money one. 90% of cheaper alternatives in Hyderabad go the WhatsApp scraping route — this plants a legitimate fear.

### Move 3: "Can you include running costs in the fee?"

**Don't say:** "Yes, let's make it ₹50k all in."
**Do say:** "I want you to see exactly what you're paying, where. If I fold running costs in, you'll never know if the price reflects true costs, and I'd have to pad it as insurance against usage spikes. Let's keep it honest — ₹45k to me, ₹1–3k/month directly to the providers."
**Why:** Transparency is the *asset* here. Hyderabad SMB owners have been burned by "all-inclusive" packages that hide margins. Your honesty is a differentiator.

### Move 4: "Let's start with a smaller pilot"

**Don't say:** "Sure, ₹10k for a pilot."
**Do say:** "Happy to. The smallest thing that proves value is: we pick your 20 most recent WhatsApp chats, run the AI over them in bulk, and I bring back a report showing intent, urgency, customer identification, and the routing each one *would* have got. That's a paid 1-week exercise at ₹8,000 — if you like the result, it credits against the main build. If you don't, you've spent ₹8,000 to find out."
**Why:** You didn't discount the real project. You offered a genuine low-risk try-before-buy. Many Hyderabad SMB owners respect this immensely — and the ones who refuse even this were never going to buy anyway.

### Move 5: "Let me talk to my CA / my partner / my brother"

**Don't say:** "Sure, take your time" (kiss of death).
**Do say:** "Absolutely — I'll send you a one-page summary right after this call, so you can forward it to whoever needs to see it. Is there a specific concern your [CA / partner / brother] tends to have, so I can address it in the summary?"
**Why:** You just un-blocked the next meeting by pre-handling the objection. And by asking who the next decision-maker is, you found out the real buying loop.

### Move 6: "What if we pay you after we see results?"

**Don't say:** "Yes" — ever.
**Do say:** "My structure is 50 / 40 / 10. You pay 50 at kickoff because that's when I start buying API credits and committing engineering time. 40 at go-live — you don't pay that until the system is actually live. 10 at end of support — if I haven't earned it, you don't pay. This way we both have skin in the game."
**Why:** You've re-anchored on a structure they can live with, without giving up the cashflow that lets you actually start.

## What to give away (gracefully)

If you sense they want a *small* win to feel good about the deal, offer one of these cheap-to-you, high-value-to-them concessions:

- **Free Loom video walkthrough** for future staff onboarding (~1 hour of your time, feels premium).
- **Free basic logo / QR poster** for their office promoting the WhatsApp number (~30 min; free goodwill).
- **Extra 7 days of support** (Option A → 52 days, Option B → 67 days).
- **First invoice exclusive of GST** if they're not GST-registered and this is a dealbreaker (~18% loss to you — avoid if possible).
- **Payment terms 40/50/10** instead of 50/40/10 (easier on their cashflow, small risk to you).

## What NEVER to give away

- Full price drop below ₹27k (Option A) or ₹40k (Option B) — signals desperation.
- Scope creep disguised as "we'll also add X while you're at it" — write it down: not in scope, extra invoice.
- Any commitment to "unlimited support" post the 45/60-day window.
- Exclusivity in Hyderabad — you want to build a portfolio.
- Unpaid pilots beyond a 2-hour discovery meeting.
- Source code ownership without a clause that lets you reuse the framework for your next client (which you will).

## Closing

- **At the end of the meeting**, ask directly: *"Based on what you've seen, is there a reason we shouldn't go ahead with Option B?"* — this is a direct close without being pushy. Whatever comes back is either an objection to handle, or a yes.
- **Follow up within 24 hours** with a 1-page SOW attached. Not 48 hours. Not 2 days. 24.
- **If they go silent**, one polite nudge after 3 days, one more after 7, then leave them be. Most deals close in the first week or not at all.

## Cultural notes specific to Hyderabad SMB owners

- **Relationship-first.** A single good meeting > a polished deck. Sit down for chai first, pitch second.
- **Referrals dominate.** Ask every client for "one other person in your industry you'd introduce me to" at go-live. Higher close rate than cold outreach 5x over.
- **Family is the board.** If they say "let me discuss at home" — they mean it. Build a 1-page summary designed to survive a dinner-table conversation.
- **Cash flow is lumpy.** They pay when they get paid. Be patient but firm on milestones.
- **English / Telugu / Hindi mix.** Match their register. Don't force English if they slip into Telugu or Hindi — they'll notice and appreciate.
- **GST awareness.** Many SMBs without GST will prefer informal receipts. *Don't compromise your own compliance* — always raise a proper invoice under TinKern Labs. Clarify the tax side up front.
