# Slide 04 — Current Pain & Bottlenecks

## Slide Title
Where your day actually hurts

## Objective
Reflect back what you heard in Slides 02 and 03, framed as the problems your solution will solve. This is the emotional pivot of the deck — the owner must nod along before you switch into "here's what we build".

## Key Points (on slide)
- **Owner bottleneck** — every decision passes through one person.
- **Manual forwarding** — messages copy-pasted to staff; context gets lost.
- **No routing** — no rule that says "area X goes to Exec A".
- **No tracking** — nobody knows if a lead was contacted, quoted, won or lost.
- **Slow follow-up** — replies depend on owner's free time.
- **Owner fatigue** — WhatsApp never closes; there is no end to the day.

## Visual
Simple "pain board" — 6 rectangles, each with an icon and a one-line symptom. Greyscale, calm, not alarmist. A thin red underline on whichever 2–3 the client strongly identified with in Slide 02.

## Speaker Notes
"Reading back what I heard — and please correct me where I am wrong — the recurring pattern is that every message, every decision, every follow-up has to cross your desk. That is fine when the business is small, but it is why even good customers sometimes feel ignored. It's not a people problem — your team is willing — it's a *system* problem. There is no system. The system right now is you. What we build on the next few slides is a system that takes the ninety-percent that doesn't need you off your plate, and leaves you with the ten-percent that actually does."

## Questions to Ask
- Out of these six, which three are most expensive to your business right now?
- Can you recall a specific lead or complaint in the last 30 days that hurt because of one of these?
- If we fixed only two of these, which two would make the biggest difference to you personally?

## What to Capture
- The three pains they rank highest — these become your proof-of-value metrics at go-live.
- The specific stories — these go into your final sales close.
- Any pain they mention that's *not* on this slide (there will usually be one; add it).
