# Slide 12 — Commercial Proposal

## Slide Title
Two ways to start — both honest, neither bloated

## Objective
Present the two priced options with clear differentiators. Hold the anchor firm; guide them to Option B. Make running-cost transparency a trust-builder, not a red flag.

## Key Points (on slide)

### Option A — Lean Build — **₹30,000 one-time**
Everything required to make the system work end-to-end for a small team.
- Discovery + architecture + WhatsApp API onboarding
- AI inbox agent (intent + urgency + TLDR)
- Lightweight data layer (customers, execs, messages, statuses)
- Routing rules + ops group notifications + owner escalation
- Testing + tuning on real data + go-live
- Written SOP & handover
- **45 days reasonable support** post go-live
- **No customer data migration**; fresh start.

### Option B — Better Build — **₹45,000 one-time**  *(recommended)*
Everything in Option A, plus:
- **One-time import** of your existing customer list (up to ~2,000 records)
- **Lightweight web admin view** (read-only): customers, execs, today's queue, statuses
- **Owner daily-digest** — a single WhatsApp/Telegram message every evening summarising the day (leads, wins/losses, pending complaints, tomorrow's bookings)
- **60 days reasonable support** post go-live

### Running costs (separate, paid by you at actuals)
- WhatsApp API (Meta): **~₹50–₹300 / mo** (most messages free in 24-hour service window)
- AI API (Gemini Flash primary): **~₹500–₹1,500 / mo**
- Hosting + database: **~₹500–₹1,500 / mo**
- **Typical total: ₹1,000–₹3,000 / mo.** Billed directly by the providers to your card / account.

### Payment terms
- 50% on kickoff (signed SOW).
- 40% on go-live.
- 10% on completion of the support period (45 or 60 days).
- Invoice raised by **TinKern Labs**. GST as applicable.

## Visual
Two clean price cards side-by-side. Option B has a "Recommended" ribbon. Below the cards, a small strip showing monthly running costs as three dots totaling "~₹1–3k/month — paid to vendors, not to us."

## Speaker Notes
"Two options. Option A gets the system live and running for thirty thousand — that's the lean build. It works. It just doesn't import your existing customer list, and there's no admin view; everything happens via WhatsApp. Option B is forty-five thousand and it adds three things that, honestly, you'll want within a few weeks of going live: we take your existing customer data and seed the system so there's continuity from day one, we build a simple web view where you or anyone with the link can see what's happening, and you get a nice end-of-day digest so you know where the business stands each evening. The running costs — WhatsApp, AI, hosting — are paid by you directly to the providers, not routed through me. I want you to see exactly what you're paying for, where. For a team your size, expect one to three thousand a month, all in."

## Questions to Ask
- Does the payment structure (50/40/10) work for you?
- Can you raise a GST invoice under *your* business name, or is this a proprietorship receipt?
- Anything in Option B that doesn't feel worth the extra 15k to you? (force them to defend A)
- If we were to add one more thing to Option B, what would be most valuable?

## What to Capture
- Their stated preference and the reason.
- Any negotiation signal — "can we do 25?" "can you include X?"
- Their billing / GST entity (needed for invoicing).

## Notes for you (not on slide)
- Hold the floor on ₹30k. Don't offer ₹25k as an opener. A ~10% discount (₹27k / ₹40k) is the maximum concession to give away if strongly pressed, and only in exchange for something (referral, case study rights, faster milestone payments). See `playbook/05-negotiation-strategy.md`.
- If they bite on Option B, do NOT immediately expand scope to prove gratitude. Scope creep starts here.
- Running costs are *explicitly not your revenue* — say it out loud; it builds enormous trust.
