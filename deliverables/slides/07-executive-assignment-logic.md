# Slide 07 — Executive Assignment Logic

## Slide Title
How messages get routed to the right person on your team

## Objective
Pin down routing rules concretely. The answer to "how do you assign jobs today" in Slide 02 feeds directly into this.

## Key Points (on slide)

- **Fixed exec per customer** — once a customer is attached to someone, they stay with that exec for continuity.
- **Area-based** — Kondapur chats → Exec A, Gachibowli chats → Exec B.
- **Service-based** — deep-cleaning → senior exec, routine → junior; office vs home; sofa vs tiles.
- **Round-robin** — balance load when no other rule applies (new leads, non-regional).
- **Owner-handled by default** — anything marked urgent / complaint / VIP routes to you in parallel.
- **Manual override** — you can always reassign via a simple WhatsApp command to the ops group.

## Visual
Decision-tree style diagram:
```
Incoming message
   │
   ▼
Is phone an existing customer?
 ├─ Yes → Route to their assigned exec
 └─ No  → Do we know the area from the message?
           ├─ Yes → Route by area rule
           └─ No  → Round-robin among available execs
                    (Owner gets a FYI copy in the ops group)
```

## Speaker Notes
"Routing is the heart of the system. We want a rule that is simple enough for you to explain to a new cleaner on day one, but smart enough that the right person always gets the right chat. My recommendation is a layered approach: if it's a returning customer, stick with the exec who served them last — continuity matters. If it's new, we try area first, then service type, then round-robin. And anything flagged urgent or a complaint — those ping you too, because frankly, complaints tend to escalate fast in this industry, and you want early warning. We will confirm the actual rules together after Slide 02's answers."

## Questions to Ask
- Do you have specific execs attached to specific areas, or is it loose?
- Do repeat customers expect the same person back?
- Which services need a senior / experienced exec vs. a junior?
- Do you have a "night / weekend" exec, or does everyone handle everything?
- Is there any customer where *you* must be the first to reply, always?

## What to Capture
- Exec names, phone numbers, their areas, their services.
- The hard rules ("VIP X always comes to me") vs soft rules ("prefer Ravi for Jubilee Hills").
- What "urgent" means in their words — write it down verbatim.
- Who covers when the primary exec is unavailable (leave, travel, etc.).
