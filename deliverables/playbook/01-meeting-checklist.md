# Meeting Checklist

A ground-level checklist for the discovery meeting with the client. Tick as you go. If you can't tick an item by end of meeting, book a follow-up specifically to close it.

## Before the meeting

- [ ] Print the client brief (`client-brief` built to PDF) — bring 2 copies.
- [ ] Bring the deck open on laptop + a backup (Google Slides / PDF on phone).
- [ ] Charge laptop, carry charger, phone hotspot ready.
- [ ] Quick dry-run of slides 1–5 (15-min owner-pain arc).
- [ ] Have a blank A4 notebook + 2 pens — you will sketch and annotate with the client.
- [ ] Check LinkedIn / Justdial / Google reviews for the business (references for later).
- [ ] Know the conversion: $1 ≈ ₹85 (April 2026) so you can convert numbers live.

## In the first 10 minutes — context

- [ ] Establish who is in the room and their role in the decision.
- [ ] Confirm the owner is present (if not, stop and reschedule — you cannot pitch this to a manager).
- [ ] Ask what's on the agenda from *their* side before launching into yours.
- [ ] Ask how much time they have — aim for 45 minutes minimum.

## Discovery — must have answers by end of meeting

### WhatsApp & tools
- [ ] Is it the WhatsApp **Business app** (green/blue) or already on WhatsApp **API** via some BSP?
- [ ] If BSP: which one, what plan, what they pay monthly, what they like/dislike.
- [ ] Number of WhatsApp accounts in use; number of phones; who has them.
- [ ] Any linked-device / WhatsApp Web usage — how many and who.
- [ ] Any CRM / field-service / scheduling tool — name, plan, monthly cost, number of users.
- [ ] Where customer names / phones actually live today (phone book / Excel / tool / nothing).

### Volume & team
- [ ] Typical messages/day (low-medium-high days). Get bands if exact numbers are hard.
- [ ] Team size — total staff, field cleaners, supervisors, back-office.
- [ ] Executive names, phones, areas, skills / service specialties.
- [ ] Hours of operation; weekend / after-hours coverage.

### Pains & stories
- [ ] Top 3 pain points in the owner's own words.
- [ ] At least one specific story of a missed lead or unhappy customer in the last 30 days.
- [ ] Their definition of "urgent" — verbatim.

### Business basics
- [ ] GST registration exists? PAN? Owner's name matches legal business name?
- [ ] Legal business name vs. brand name — any mismatch?
- [ ] Do they have a website / social handles (needed for Meta verification)?

## Architecture decisions — soft agreement by end of meeting

- [ ] Keep current WhatsApp number or new? (default: keep current)
- [ ] Fresh data / one-time import / live sync? (default: one-time import if clean list)
- [ ] Assignment logic: fixed / area / hybrid? (default: hybrid — see Slide 07)
- [ ] Routing pattern: group+tags or shared inbox? (default: group+tags)
- [ ] Option A (₹30k) or Option B (₹45k)? (default: B is recommended)

## Close

- [ ] Reconfirm the 7 decisions list from Slide 14.
- [ ] Commit to a 24-hour follow-up (SOW or meeting notes).
- [ ] Ask for permission to reach their GST / PAN / business verification contact (often a CA).
- [ ] Ask if you can quietly observe their current WhatsApp for an hour (huge value — see `02-inspection-guide.md`).
- [ ] Exchange business cards / save contact properly.

## After — same day

- [ ] Send a thank-you note within 2 hours with a short summary.
- [ ] Send the one-page printable brief as PDF attachment.
- [ ] Block time on your calendar for the SOW draft within 24 hours.
- [ ] Update CRM / tracker with everything captured.

## Red flag during meeting — stop and reassess

See `03-red-flags-and-gaps.md`. If any appear, do not discount to save the sale. Walk or renegotiate.
